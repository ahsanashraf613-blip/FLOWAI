router.ts
TypeScript 
import { flow, route } from '@flowai/sdk';

const pipeline = flow.create({
  name: 'smart-router',
  input: route.smart({
    models: ['gpt-4o', 'claude-3.5'],
    strategy: 'cost-optimal',
    fallback: true,
  }),
  output: flow.stream({ every: 16 }),
});

await pipeline.deploy({ edge: 'global' });

/ Architecture

How a FLOWAI pipeline executes.

STEP 01

Ingestion

Request hits the nearest edge node. Authentication, rate limiting, and schema validation happen instantly.

STEP 02

Routing

The router evaluates context, cost, and latency to select the optimal model node for the request.

STEP 03

Execution

Data flows through transformations and model calls. Retries and fallbacks trigger if errors occur.

STEP 04

Stream & Trace

Results are streamed back to the client. Every step is traced and logged for observability.

  name: 'playground-export',

Frequently asked questions

How are requests counted?

A request is a single execution of your pipeline. If your pipeline calls 3 models internally, it still counts as 1 request.

Do I pay for the underlying models?

No. FLOWAI handles all model provider costs. You only pay your flat monthly fee — no usage-based surprise bills.

Can I self-host FLOWAI?

Yes. Enterprise customers can deploy FLOWAI on their own VPC. Contact our sales team for details.

What happens if I hit my limit?

We'll notify you at 80% and 100% usage. You can upgrade instantly, or requests will be queued until the next cycle.

Do you support custom models?

Yes. You can add custom nodes for your internal models hosted on Replicate, HuggingFace, or your own endpoints.
